I am soon-to-be graduate of CSU San Bernardino's Computer Science program, which taught me a wonderful breadth of critical thinking, problem solving, and practical programming skills. However, one of the things I wish I knew more about, as a computer scientist, was web design and development. I'd like to say this entire site was built from sheer willpower, but I had help, both from fellow students and free and open source code resources.

Here is an incomplete listing and brief description of some of the more important resources I used to build this site.

  • Andrew Brinker and his Front End Field Guide gave me a marvelous theoretical grounding and wealth of resources. Advice from him helped shape and personalize the site.
  • The Mozilla Developer Network was my go-to reference for HTML, CSS, and JavaScript.
  • HTML5 Boilerplate provides a marvelous jumping off point, licensed as free software under the MIT license.

And more will be added as I continue to develop the site!
